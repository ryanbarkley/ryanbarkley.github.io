Staying up-to-date

Our industry is constantly changing, which can make it challenging to stay up-to-date on the latest trends and best practices. Below is a curated collection of some of my favorite industry resources I use to stay up-to-date aside from who I’m currently following on Twitter. (Following a bunch of smart people in the tech industry is one of the primary ways I passively stay up on the latest news and technologies.)

Podcasts

  • ThoughtWorks Technology Podcast
  • O11ycast
  • Maintainable
  • Software Engineering Daily
  • Down the Security Rabbit Hole
  • The Heartbeat ❤️ Podcast

Blogs

  • Signal v. Noise

Tools

  • ThoughtWorks Technology Radar

Thinking big-picture

It’s easy to get lost in the day-to-day ideas and trends, so it’s important to keep the big picture in mind and challenge the status quo to avoid groupthink. Below are a few resources I’ve found helpful to that end.

Books

  • The Phoenix Project: A Novel about IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win by Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, and George Spafford
  • The Unicorn Project: A Novel about Developers, Digital Disruption, and Thriving in the Age of Data by Gene Kim
  • An Elegant Puzzle: Systems of Engineering Management by Will Larson
  • Turn the Ship Around!: A True Story of Turning Followers into Leaders by L. David Marquet
  • It Doesn’t Have to Be Crazy at Work by Jason Fried and David Heinemeier Hansson

Diving deeper

While it’s important to learn broad concepts and gain big-picture context, sometimes you need to dive deep into specific subjects. Below are some resources I’ve found helpful in gaining a more throrough understanding of specific technical concepts and problem domains.

Books

  • Building Microservices: Designing Fine-Grained Systems by Sam Newman
  • Monolith to Microservices: Evolutionary Patterns to Transform Your Monolith by Sam Newman

Websites

  • martinfowler.com
  • The Architect Elevator

Building a career

As with many things in life, a bit of intentionality goes a long way when it comes to designing a career. Below are some resources I’ve found helpful in expanding my own mindset when it comes to career growth and objectives.

Books

  • The Making of a Manager: What to Do When Everyone Looks to You by Julie Zhuo
  • The Manager’s Path: A Guide for Tech Leaders Navigating Growth and Change by Camille Fournier

Articles

  • “The Engineer/Manager Pendulum” by Charity Majors
  • “Don’t Call Yourself a Programmer” by Patrick McKenzie
